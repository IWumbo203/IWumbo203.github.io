BIBLIOGRAPHY AND RESOURCES

Here are some good books and resources for information about us. (Millie)

Books

  • Barlett, R.D. and Patricia A., Frogs, Toads and Treefrogs,Barron's Educational Series, 250 Wireless Blvd., Hauppage, NY 11788, 1996

  • Coburn, John, The Proper Care of Amphibians, T.F.H. Publications Inc.,211 West Sylvania Ave., Neptune City, NJ 07753 1992

  • Grenard, Steve, Frogs and Toads, (An Owner's Guide to a Happy Healthy Pet), Howell Book House (Simon & Schuster Macmillan), 1633 Broadway, New York, NY 11788 1998

  • Pyrom, Jay, Frogs and Toads (A Complete Introduction),T.F.H. Publications, 211 West Sylvania Ave., Neptune City, NJ 07753 1987

Periodicals

  • Reptiles (Guide to Keeping Reptiles and Amphibians), P.O. Box 58700, Boulder, CO 80322-8700 (800) 365-4421

  • Reptile Hobbyist, T.F.H. Publications, Inc., One TFH Plaza, Neptune City, NJ 07753  (908) 988-8400

  • Reptile and Amphibian Magazine,RD3, Box 3709-A, Pottsville, PA 17901

  • The Vivarium (The Publication of the American Federation of Herpetoculturists), P.O. Box 300067, Escondido, CA 92030

    This list is by no means exhaustive. There are many books devoted to a specific frog as well. The library can provide many reference books and your local pet store will most certainly have books and periodicals to assist you in a better understanding of your frog so that you can provide him (or her as the case may be) the best environment you can. There may be a Herpetological society in your community which is also a valuable resource.
